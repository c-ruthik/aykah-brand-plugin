## Voice Rules — Four Core Attributes

1. **Natural & Conversational** — like a founder explaining something clearly. Not a marketing department.
2. **Confident but Understated** — quality is implied. Confidence comes from specifics, not adjectives.
3. **Warm but Not Emotional** — human, not theatrical. Reads well at a kitchen table, not a runway.
4. **Design-aware but Not Pretentious** — we know our materials. We don't talk down.

### The Voice Spectrum

| We are | We are not |
|---|---|
| Warm & welcoming | Stiff or corporate |
| Confident & knowledgeable | Pushy or salesy |
| Premium-feeling | Pretentious or exclusive |
| Helpful & educational | Condescending or dumbed-down |
| Honest & transparent | Vague or full of marketing fluff |
| Inspiring & creative | Bland or generic |

### Emphasize vs. Avoid

**Emphasize:** how furniture fits into everyday life · function and design working together · calm, organized, intentional spaces · subtle details that make a difference · practical benefits without sounding technical.

**Avoid:** clichés ("timeless elegance," "elevated living") · overly emotional or poetic language · aggressive sales tone · overly technical/clinical explanations · comparing to competitors by name.

## Language Swaps (instead of → write)

| Instead of | Write | Why |
|---|---|---|
| "Free shipping" | Complimentary delivery across Canada | "Complimentary" = gift from brand. Burberry uses this. |
| "Luxury furniture" | Finely made / Well-designed | We don't claim luxury — we prove quality with materials. |
| "High-quality materials" | Name the actual material | Every brand says "high-quality." Specificity builds trust. |
| "Cheap" / "Budget" | Remove entirely | Incompatible with premium positioning. |
| "Buy now!" | Explore / Discover the collection | We invite, we don't pressure. |
| "Comfortable" | Describe the sensation | "Sink-in support" beats "comfortable." |
| "Durable / Strong" | Engineered for longevity | Frames lifespan, not resistance. |
| "Best-seller" | Signature piece / Most coveted | Mass popularity ≠ exclusivity. |
| "Customer service" | Client care | Elevates the relationship. |
| "No assembly required!" | Arrives fully assembled | Statement of fact, not excited relief. |
| $899.99 | $900 (round numbers) | Round numbers = premium (Zhang & Wadhwa, 2015). |
| "Don't pay for the markup" | We charge for the wood, not the words | Original is dig-y. Replacement is belief-coded. |

## Premium Specification Language

When describing construction, **don't be generic — name the technique and certification.**

| Don't say | Say this | Certification |
|---|---|---|
| Wood frame | Kiln-dried hardwood frame — heat-treated to reduce moisture, prevent warping | FSC-certified |
| Strong joints | Corner-blocked, dowelled joinery | — |
| Good cushions | Web suspension system — distributes weight evenly, reduces sagging | — |
| Springs | Sinuous spring suspension — responsive support through 10,000+ compressions | — |
| Foam | High-density foam, CertiPUR-US certified | CertiPUR-US |
| Nice fabric | 280 GSM performance bouclé, 50K+ Martindale cycles | OEKO-TEX Standard 100 |
| Piping | Self-welt construction — tailored finish in matching fabric | — |
| Adjustable feet | Precision levelers for stability on any surface | — |
| Metal legs | Brushed brass-finished steel with electroplated coating | — |

### Wood terms (from Product team)

`Open grain · Straight grain · Fine grained · Natural knots · Exposed peg detailing · Wire brushed · Kiln-dried · Dovetail construction`

### Certifications — how to present

**Premium way (do this):**
- "Frame: FSC-certified hardwood — sourced from responsibly managed forests"
- "Foam: CertiPUR-US certified — tested for harmful chemicals, emissions, and durability"
- "Textile: OEKO-TEX Standard 100 — screened against 1,000+ harmful substances"
- "Finish: GREENGUARD Gold — meets strict chemical emission limits"

**Never:**
- "Eco-friendly materials" / "Safe and tested" / "Green certified" / "Environmentally conscious" — all vague. Name the specific certification.

## Product Description Template

3-part framework. **200–400 words per product.** Voice doesn't change between price points — materials and craft do.

| Part | Words | What it does |
|---|---|---|
| **Part 1 — The Hook** | 80–120 | How does it feel in a room? Sell the lifestyle. Lead with emotion, follow with one material detail. |
| **Part 2 — The Specs** | 60–100 | Bullet points. Dimensions, materials, features, suspension, certifications. Be exact. Use premium terms. |
| **Part 3 — The Care** | 60–80 | Cleaning tips, care frequency, what to avoid. Practical and brief. |

### Example — current tier ($579 Storage Bed)

> AURA LIFT-UP STORAGE BED — QUEEN · $579
>
> "Solid wood frame. Linen-upholstered headboard. Two underbed storage drawers on soft-close runners. Designed for everyday living, with durability that holds up over time. Quietly better."

### Example — premium tier ($1,899 Storage Bed, Fall 2026)

> AURA KING STORAGE BED — KING · $1,900
>
> "Kiln-dried solid hardwood frame. Belgian linen headboard with hand-tufted detailing. Four soft-close drawers. CertiPUR-US certified foam headboard padding. Precision levelers on each leg. Built to last decades, not seasons. Quietly better."

**Note:** voice stays the same. Materials and craft details scale with price. Price reads as a function of what's *in* the piece.

## Channel Guide

Same voice, adjusted warmth. Use the right hook for each channel.

| Channel | Warmth | Formality | Primary hook(s) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Product Pages | Medium | Medium | Sub-line + materials hook |
| Blog Posts | High | Low–Medium | Living hook + craft hook |
| Social Media | High | Low | Materials hook + living hook |
| Email Promos | Medium–High | Low–Medium | Sub-line + product specifics |
| Customer Service | Very High | Medium | Service hook |
| About / Brand | High | Medium | Anti-markup hook + voice tag |
| Paid Social | Medium | Low | "Belgian linen. Solid oak. $1,299." format |
| Reels / TikTok | High | Low | Materials hook + craft hook. ASMR audio. |
| Showroom Signage | Medium | Medium | Materials hook + tagline |
| Announcement Bar | Low | Medium | Rotate 3 messages, 5–7 sec intervals |

### Announcement bar — 3 rotating messages

1. Complimentary delivery across Canada on orders over $199
2. Welcome to the new aykah.ca — explore our collections
3. 1-year warranty on all furniture

### Social media caption formula

```
[Hook — scene or emotion] →
[Product + one material detail] →
[Brand positioning] →
[Soft CTA] →
[3–5 hashtags]
```

**Example:**
> "Morning light, warm coffee, nowhere else you'd rather be. The Enigma Sofa in oat linen — 280 GSM performance fabric, built on a kiln-dried hardwood frame. Quietly better. Explore at aykah.ca. #AykahHome #ModernFurnitureCanada"

### Email subject lines

**Write:** "Introducing the new collection" · "How we choose our oak" · "Your new sofa — a care guide" · "We've been working on something"

**Never:** ALL CAPS subjects · exclamation marks · emojis · "Last chance!" · "Don't miss out!"

### Customer service response rule

**Acknowledge first, solve second.** Use customer's first name. Be transparent about policies. End with next steps, not dead ends.

> "Hi Sarah, thank you for reaching out. I'm sorry the delivery took longer than expected — that's not the experience we want for you. I've looked into your order and here's what's happening…"

## Hard checks before publishing

1. Run `bun scripts/voice-lint.ts <file>` — must pass with zero errors.
2. Cross-check facts against `brand-facts.md` — never invent claims.
3. Tone passes the four-attribute test (natural / confident / warm / design-aware).
4. No exclamation marks (rare exceptions only).
5. Numbers, prices, and policies match verified facts.
6. Right hook for the channel (see Channel Guide above).
