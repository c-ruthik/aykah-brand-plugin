# Method 1 — Frame the Problem

**Stack:** 5 Whys + How Might We (HMW) + First Principles
**Source research:** P&G / IDEO (HMW), Toyota Production System (5 Whys), Aristotle / Musk (first principles)

---

## What it does

Forces problem-framing before any solution-generation. Most brainstorms produce mediocre ideas because they ideate the wrong problem.

## When to use

- At the start of any brainstorm where the problem isn't crisp
- "Give me ideas for X" with no underlying clarity
- "I'm stuck" / "we keep going in circles"
- Whenever someone jumps straight to solutions

## Workflow

### Step 1 — 5 Whys

Take the stated problem. Ask "why?" five times in sequence, each answer becoming the new problem. The 5th why is usually a different problem than the 1st.

**Aykah example:** "Aires Dining Chair listing isn't converting" → why? CTR is fine but PDP bounces → why? Photos look generic → why? Factory shots, no lifestyle context → why? No process for Amazon visuals → why? Nobody owns Amazon visual production. **Real problem:** ownership, not the listing.

### Step 2 — Generate 5–10 How Might We (HMW) questions

The phrasing matters: *how* implies a solution exists, *might* keeps options open, *we* makes it collaborative.

Vary the framing axis — at minimum:

- One narrow and tactical
- One broad and strategic
- One that flips the problem
- One that questions whether the goal is even right
- One that targets a different stakeholder

### Step 3 — First Principles check

For the chosen HMW, strip out assumptions and rebuild from fundamentals:

- What do we *know* is true (verified, not assumed)?
- What are we assuming that might not be true?
- If we ignore industry conventions, what does the problem actually require?

This step often kills the chosen HMW and surfaces a better one. That's a feature, not a bug.

## Output format

```
Original stated problem: ...
5 Whys chain: 1.→ 2.→ 3.→ 4.→ 5.
Root-cause problem: <one sentence>
HMW options:
  - <tactical>
  - <strategic>
  - <inversion>
  - <goal-questioning>
  - <stakeholder-shift>
Recommended HMW: ...
First-principles check:
  - Verified: ...
  - Assumed: ...
  - Implications: ...
Final framed problem: <one clean HMW>
Maturity: Refined
```

After framing, route to the next method (Diverge / Lotus / TRIZ depending on shape).

## Common failure modes

- Skipping straight to ideas before framing is done
- Surface-level Whys that all give similar answers (you're not drilling)
- HMWs that are solutions in disguise ("HMW use Instagram ads" — that's already an answer)
- Stopping at HMW without the First Principles pass
