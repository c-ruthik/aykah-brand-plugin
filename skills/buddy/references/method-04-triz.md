# Method 4 — TRIZ Contradiction

**Source:** Genrikh Altshuller (Soviet Union, 1946–1985). Theory of Inventive Problem Solving. fNIRS brain imaging shows TRIZ produces sustained PFC activation vs. brainstorming's early spike + fade — suggesting deeper cognitive engagement.

---

## What it does

Resolves tradeoffs by **dissolving** the contradiction rather than splitting the difference. Built on the insight that most "tradeoffs" aren't physical necessities — they're conventions.

## When to use

Whenever there's a "we want X but Y" problem:

- "Faster shipping but lower cost"
- "More SKUs but less inventory risk"
- "Premium feel but accessible price"
- "Autonomous AI workflow but reliable output"
- "More personalization but lower production cost"

## Workflow

### Step 1 — Name the contradiction explicitly

Format: **"We want [improving feature] without sacrificing [degrading feature]."**

If you can't write it cleanly in this format, it's not a contradiction — it's an unresolved divergence task. Route back to Method 1 (Frame).

### Step 2 — Reality check

Before applying principles, interrogate:

- Is this a physical necessity, or a convention?
- Who has already broken this tradeoff (even partially)?
- What's the assumed mechanism that links the two sides?

About 30% of contradictions dissolve here. If yours does, name the dissolved framing and stop.

### Step 3 — Apply 5–8 of the most relevant Inventive Principles

Don't try to memorize all 40. The most useful for furniture / e-commerce / content / AI work:

| # | Principle | Reads as... |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Segmentation | Divide into independent parts (sectionals split into modules; AI pipeline into specialized agents) |
| 2 | Taking out | Remove the problematic part (skip the Critic step if only 10% of outputs need correction) |
| 3 | Local quality | Different parts have different properties (premium feel on visible faces, cheaper on hidden) |
| 5 | Merging | Combine identical or related operations (Burnaby + Mississauga events same weekend share marketing spend) |
| 10 | Preliminary action | Do as much as possible in advance (pre-render image batches; pre-sell limited runs) |
| 13 | Inversion | Invert the action or the order (customer photographs the product, brand reposts, instead of staging) |
| 15 | Dynamicity | Make a fixed thing flexible (modular content templates instead of one-off campaigns) |
| 17 | Another dimension | 1D to 2D, 2D to 3D (email isn't your only channel; SMS + email + DM is a different dimension) |
| 20 | Continuous useful action | Eliminate idle phases (a pipeline that produces while you sleep) |
| 22 | Convert harm into benefit | Use a negative as a positive (delivery wait time becomes "build anticipation" content arc) |
| 24 | Intermediary | Introduce a temporary intermediate step (human-in-the-loop sanity check between stages) |
| 25 | Self-service | The system serves itself (customers self-style with AR; warehouse auto-reconciles) |
| 26 | Copying | Cheap simple copy instead of expensive original (AI-generated context vs. expensive photoshoots) |
| 28 | Mechanics substitution | Replace one mechanism with another (mechanical → electrical → optical → digital) |
| 35 | Parameter changes | Change physical state, concentration, format (same content, different format/length per channel) |
| 37 | Thermal expansion | Exploit gradual scaling (soft launch to A-List, expand outward in waves) |
| 40 | Composite materials | Multiple components combined (brand kit = serif + sans + warm gold + lower-case "a") |

### Step 4 — Apply each principle to the contradiction

Write one sentence per principle explaining what it would imply. Many will be nonsensical — that's expected. Two or three produce real insight.

### Step 5 — Pick the strongest principle(s) and write the resolution

State the resolved approach as one sentence. Note which principles produced it. Identify what would have to be true for it to work.

## Output format

```
Contradiction: We want <X> without sacrificing <Y>
Reality check:
  - Necessity or convention?
  - Who's already broken this?
  - Assumed mechanism: ...
[If dissolved here, stop with the dissolved framing.]

Principles applied (5–8):
  - #N <Principle name>: <one-sentence implication>
  - ...

Strongest resolution: <one sentence>
Source principles: #N, #M
Conditions for it to work: <bullet list>
Maturity: Developing
```

After TRIZ, route to **Method 5 — Pressure-Test** on the resolution.

## Common failure modes

- Vague contradiction statement ("we want better customer experience" is not a contradiction)
- Skipping the reality check
- Settling for "we'll meet in the middle" (TRIZ rejects this — that's compromise, not resolution)
- Treating principle names too literally ("thermal expansion" means gradual scaling, not heat)
