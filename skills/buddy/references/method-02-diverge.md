# Method 2 — Diverge

**Stack:** Crazy 8s (default) + Brainwriting + SCAMPER + Worst Possible Idea + Random Stimulus
**Source research:** IDEO / Google Ventures Design Sprint (Crazy 8s), Bob Eberle 1971 (SCAMPER), Diehl & Stroebe meta-analyses (Brainwriting > group brainstorming, ~40% more viable ideas)

---

## What it does

Generates volume and variety. No filtering, no judgment, no recommendations. Includes deliberately uncomfortable ideas.

## When to use

After framing is done, when you need ideas — campaigns, content angles, product variations, tactics, options.

## Hard rules

- Quantity over quality
- No filtering during generation
- No "I'd recommend X" — that's convergence
- Always include 2–3 ideas that feel wrong or uncomfortable
- No caveats ("this might not work but...") — just list it

## Five techniques (pick one based on situation)

### A. Crazy 8s (default)

Generate exactly 8 ideas, one sentence each, with forced variety:

1. The obvious one
2. Tactical, near-term
3. Strategic, long-term
4. Copy a different industry
5. Inversion of the problem
6. Unlimited budget version
7. Zero budget version
8. Deliberately uncomfortable

### B. Brainwriting

When the user has shared 1–3 ideas first, build on each with 5 more in three modes:

- **Build** — extend the idea further in the same direction
- **Contradict** — reverse it; what's the opposite version?
- **Orthogonal** — different approach to the same goal

Sequencing matters: user ideas first prevents anchoring.

### C. SCAMPER

For iterating on an existing thing. Generate ≥1 idea per letter:

- **S — Substitute** — what could replace a component?
- **C — Combine** — what could merge with this?
- **A — Adapt** — what could be borrowed from another context?
- **M — Modify / Magnify** — what could be exaggerated or scaled?
- **P — Put to other use** — who else could use this?
- **E — Eliminate** — what could be removed?
- **R — Reverse** — what could be flipped?

### D. Worst Possible Idea

Generate 5–8 deliberately terrible ideas. The point isn't humor — it's removing the politeness filter. Then invert each: bad-version-flipped often reveals the good version faster than asking directly.

### E. Random Stimulus / Forced Analogy

Pick a random word, object, or industry. Force a connection to the problem. For each random anchor, generate 3 ideas. Rotate anchors:

- Industries: hotels, hospitals, magazines, museums, airlines, dive bars, libraries, farmers' markets
- Objects: lighthouse, vending machine, paperback novel, postcard, Swiss army knife
- Concepts: gravity, recursion, low tide, shadow, pilgrimage

## Output format

```
Method: Diverge — <technique used>
Framed problem: <from Method 1, or restate>
Ideas:
  1. <one sentence>     [Maturity: Raw]
  2. ...
  ...
Watch out for: <banned words / clichés to avoid in this set, e.g. "elevated living", "luxury experience">
Note: 2–3 of the above are deliberately uncomfortable. Keep them in.
```

After diverging, route to **Method 8 — Converge** to narrow.

## Common failure modes

- Producing 5 ideas when the target was 8 (the Crazy 8 number is intentional — at 5 you're still in safe territory)
- All ideas in the same neighborhood (force the variety constraints)
- Adding evaluation language during generation ("this is risky but...")
- Skipping the deliberately uncomfortable slots
