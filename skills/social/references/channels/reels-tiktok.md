# Reels & TikTok

Channel dial (from Voice Guide v2): **High warmth, Low formality, Materials hook + Craft hook + ASMR audio.**

These are scripts, not captions. Think shot list with timestamps.

---

## Length

- **Sweet spot:** 15–30 seconds.
- **Reels:** up to 90 seconds, but engagement drops sharply past 30s.
- **TikTok:** up to 3 min, but Aykah's furniture content rarely needs more than 30s.
- **First 3 seconds decide everything.** If the hook doesn't land in 0:03, the rest doesn't matter.

---

## The four-beat structure

```
0:00 – 0:03   HOOK         (visual + audio attention-grab)
0:03 – 0:10   MATERIAL     (the detail — close-up + name it)
0:10 – 0:25   CONTEXT      (the room, the moment, the use)
0:25 – 0:30   SOFT CTA     (where to see / shop)
```

For shorter content (15s), compress: 0–3 hook, 3–10 material, 10–15 soft cta. Skip the context beat if the visual already provides it.

---

## The Hook (0:00–0:03)

The hook must work with **sound off** AND **sound on** simultaneously.

- **Visual:** something happening — a hand, a press, a slide, a pour. Static scenes lose.
- **On-screen text:** one short line, max 6 words. If a still frame at 0:01 doesn't carry the message, the hook is broken.
- **Audio:** ASMR-style native sound (the linen rubbing, the drawer closing, the wood creaking softly) — Voice Guide v2 calls this out specifically.

### Hook formats that work for furniture

- **Sensory close-up:** the hand pressing into a cushion, the linen weave catching light, the drawer sliding closed
- **Reveal:** wide shot to detail (room → sofa → fabric → thread)
- **Sound-led:** lead with audio (the click of a soft-close drawer), camera follows
- **Process:** something being made, finished, or installed
- **Before/after:** room transition (rare — only if it's genuinely good)

### Hook formats that don't work

- Talking-head intros ("Hi guys, today I'm showing you...")
- Text-only opener with no visual movement
- Logo-first reveals (save for end)
- Generic B-roll of a styled room (everyone has those)

---

## On-screen text rules

- Max 6 words per frame
- Manrope sans-serif (matches brand typography)
- Lowercase or sentence case — never ALL CAPS
- Position: lower-third or center; never overlap product
- Stay on screen at least 1.5 seconds (readability minimum)

---

## ASMR audio is the differentiator

Voice Guide v2 prescribes ASMR audio for this channel. That means:

- Native captured sound on every beat — fabric, wood, paper, a soft press
- Music is secondary, low in the mix, used sparingly
- No royalty-free corporate music. No trending-audio bait that has nothing to do with the product.
- If the audio doesn't reinforce the material story, it's wrong

---

## Caption (under the video)

The caption is short — most viewers won't read past the hook.

- **30–80 words max**
- Same formula as IG feed: scene/emotion → material detail → soft positioning → soft CTA
- 2–3 hashtags (TikTok prefers fewer than IG)

---

## Output format for Reels/TikTok

```
PILLAR: <Materials / Craft / Living / Pricing / Service / Voice / Campaign>
LENGTH: <15s / 20s / 30s / 60s>

CAPTION:
<short caption, scene + material + soft CTA>

HASHTAGS:
<2–3 tags, channel-appropriate>

SHOT SCRIPT:

00:00–00:03   HOOK
  Visual: <what's on screen>
  On-screen text: "<6 words max>"
  Audio: <ASMR / native sound description>

00:03–00:10   MATERIAL
  Visual: <close-up detail>
  On-screen text: "<material name + one detail>"
  Audio: <native sound>

00:10–00:25   CONTEXT
  Visual: <wider shot, room or hand-on-product>
  On-screen text: <optional, only if it adds>
  Audio: <native sound, optional low music bed>

00:25–00:30   SOFT CTA
  Visual: <product still or logo end-card>
  On-screen text: "explore the collection"
  Audio: <fade>

VOICE-GATE: APPROVED
PERSONAS: 3/3 passed
```

---

## Hashtags (Reels/TikTok)

- **Reels:** 2–3 tags max — IG indexes a 5-cap, but Reels engagement is content-driven not tag-driven
- **TikTok:** 3–5 tags, broad + niche mix (`#FurnitureTok` is fine; `#LivingRoom` is broad-reach; `#KilnDriedOak` is niche)

Same Aykah pool as Instagram (see `instagram.md`). Don't introduce TikTok-specific brand tags unless brand approves.

---

## What kills a Reel

- Talking-head opener
- Music that has nothing to do with the product
- Generic styled-room B-roll with no detail
- More than 6 words on screen at once
- Product shot held static for more than 4 seconds without movement
- Voiceover narrating what we can already see ("This is a beautiful sofa")

## Example — PASS

**Pillar:** Materials
**Length:** 15s

**Caption:**
> The way Belgian linen catches afternoon light. Aura sectional, oat colorway. Made of what matters.
>
> Explore the collection. Link in bio.
>
> #AykahHome #BelgianLinen

**Shot script:**

```
00:00–00:03   HOOK
  Visual: Hand presses into linen cushion, slow release
  On-screen text: "the way linen softens"
  Audio: Soft press, fabric give

00:03–00:08   MATERIAL
  Visual: Macro close-up — weave catching window light
  On-screen text: "Belgian linen. 280 GSM."
  Audio: Native (ambient room)

00:08–00:13   CONTEXT
  Visual: Pulls back — sectional in afternoon room
  Audio: Light music bed enters, low

00:13–00:15   SOFT CTA
  Visual: Aykah wordmark, oat-on-ivory
  On-screen text: "explore the collection"
  Audio: Fade
```
