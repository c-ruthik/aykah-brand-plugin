# Instagram (Feed)

Channel dial (from Voice Guide v2): **High warmth, Low formality, Materials hook + Living hook.**

---

## Caption structure (Voice Guide formula, applied)

```
[Hook — scene or emotion]
->
[Product + one material detail]
->
[Brand positioning — light touch]
->
[Soft CTA]
->
[Hashtags]
```

### Length

- **First 125 characters** must work in isolation. After 125 chars, IG truncates with "...more". Anything important goes above the fold.
- **Total caption:** 30–150 words is the sweet spot. Up to 2,200 chars allowed but rarely useful.
- **Two-tier rule:** scroll-stopper line (under 125 chars) + 2–4 sentences below for those who tap "more". Don't fight this — design for it.

### Hook types that work

- A scene — "Sunday morning, second cup."
- A material observation — "The linen weave softens after the third wash."
- A small sensory detail — "The sound a solid oak drawer makes when it slides shut."
- A line that reads like a thought, not a headline

### What does NOT work

- Generic "Living room goals" / "Home inspo" / "Home of dreams"
- Questions to drive engagement ("Which one is your favorite?")
- Lists ("3 reasons you'll love this sofa") — that's blog post energy, not feed
- Anything that needs an exclamation to feel like it has stakes

## Hashtags

**2026 IG indexes a hard maximum of 5 hashtags per post.** Adding more dilutes reach.

- **Count:** 3–5 hashtags. No more.
- **Mix:** 1 broad-reach + 2 brand/category + 1–2 niche
- **Placement:** in the caption (not the first comment) — IG's 2026 algorithm prefers in-caption
- **Style:** lowercase or CamelCase, both fine; pick one and stay consistent within a post

## Alt-text

Required on every IG post for accessibility + SEO indexing.

**Formula:** `[Product Name] – [Material/Color] – [Angle/View] – [Context]`

Example: `Aura Sectional in oat-colored Belgian linen – three-quarter view – modern living room with low afternoon light`

Keep alt-text **literal and descriptive**, not poetic. Screen readers parse it; SEO crawlers parse it. Save the brand voice for the caption.

---

## First comment

Use the first comment for:

- Care instructions
- Material certifications (CertiPUR-US, OEKO-TEX, FSC) — see brand-voice.md "Premium way" section
- Cross-references to other products in the room
- Showroom address(es) where the piece can be seen in person

**Don't** put hashtags in the first comment in 2026 — IG indexes in-caption tags.

---

## Carousels (multi-image posts)

- **First image:** the hero scene. The reason someone stops scrolling.
- **Image 2–3:** material detail close-ups. Show the weave, the grain, the joint.
- **Image 4–5:** lifestyle / context. Person interacting (hands, a leg, never face-front modeling).
- **Last image:** product on plain background OR specs card.

Carousel captions can run longer than single-image (people who swipe will read).

---

## Posting cadence (rough guidance, not rule)

- 3–5 feed posts per week
- 1–2 Stories per day
- 2–4 Reels per week (see `reels-tiktok.md`)

## Examples

### PASS — feed post (single image, hero shot of sectional)

> Sunday morning, second cup. The Aura sectional in oat linen — Belgian weave, kiln-dried oak frame, low and deep enough to settle into.
>
> Made of what matters.
>
> Explore the collection. Link in bio.
>
> #AykahHome #BelgianLinen #ModernFurnitureCanada

### FAIL — same post, off-brand

> Introducing the gorgeous new Aura Sectional! Featuring stunning Belgian linen and a durable solid wood frame, this piece is the perfect addition to any modern home. Don't miss out — get yours today!
>
> #LuxuryLiving #ElevatedHome #HomeInspo #LivingRoomGoals #FurnitureSale #ShopNow #Trending #Style

**Why it fails:** corporate filler ("Introducing", "gorgeous", "stunning", "perfect"), urgency ("Don't miss out"), banned phrasing ("Luxury Living", "Elevated Home"), 8 hashtags (IG caps at 5), exclamation marks, no material specificity, no scene hook.
