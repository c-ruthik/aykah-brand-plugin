# Anti-Patterns — what NOT to write

Two categories: AI rhythm tells (the cadence that signals machine output) + Aykah-specific brand drift.

The voice-gate agent catches the banned-words list mechanically. The three personas catch tone drift. This file is the third layer — the rhythm and structure tells.

---

## AI rhythm tells (refuse to write these)

### 1. Tricolons (three-part rule of three)

Bad: "Beautiful, durable, and built to last."
Why: AI defaults to threes. Real writing varies.
Fix: Pick one detail, name it precisely. "Built to last decades."

### 2. Em-dash overuse

Bad: "The Aura sectional — built for modern living — is now in stock."
Why: AI loves the parenthetical em-dash. Humans use it sparingly.
Fix: Two short sentences. "The Aura sectional. Now in stock."

### 3. "It's not just X — it's Y"

Bad: "It's not just a sofa. It's where you'll spend Sunday."
Why: This pattern is in every AI ad spec. Killed.
Fix: Just describe Y. "Where you'll spend Sunday."

### 4. "In a world where..."

Bad: "In a world where furniture is disposable, Aykah is built to last."
Why: Movie trailer cadence. Never a real sentence.
Fix: Drop the framing entirely. "Built to last."

### 5. "Imagine..."

Bad: "Imagine sinking into the Aura sectional after a long day."
Why: Doesn't trust the reader.
Fix: Show the scene. "The end of a long day. The Aura sectional, deep oat linen."

### 6. Question-as-hook

Bad: "Looking for a sectional that fits your space?"
Why: Patronizing and generic. Real writing leads with a statement.
Fix: Lead with the answer or the moment.

### 7. "Crafted with [vague claim]"

Bad: "Crafted with the finest materials."
Why: Says nothing. AI fallback when it doesn't have a real material name.
Fix: Name the material. "Belgian linen. Kiln-dried white oak."

### 8. Adjective stacks

Bad: "Beautiful, stunning, luxurious, modern oak coffee table."
Why: Every adjective dilutes the next. AI piles them up.
Fix: One specific detail. "Solid white oak. Live edge."

### 9. Generic emotion-baiting

Bad: "Live your best life with Aykah."
Why: Stock phrase, says nothing, blends with every other brand.
Fix: Show the actual living. "For the kind of evening that doesn't have a plan."

### 10. "Welcome to..."

Bad: "Welcome to the new Aura collection."
Why: Press-release cadence, not social.
Fix: Just state it. "The Aura collection, now at aykah.ca."

## Aykah-specific brand drift (refuse to write these)

### "Luxury" / "Premium" / "Elevated" / "Timeless"

Already in the banned list. The personas catch them in fresh combinations too:
- "Elevated everyday living"
- "Timeless modern silhouettes"
- "Premium materials at accessible price points"

All FAIL. Use specific material/craft language instead. See Voice Guide v2 Section 5.

### Discount-store rhythm

Even without banned words, sometimes the *cadence* is wrong:

Bad: "New collection. Available now. Free delivery on orders over $199."
Why: Reads like a Boxing Day flyer. The brand voice is conversational, not announcement-board.
Fix: "The Aura collection. Now in oat and slate. Complimentary delivery across Canada over $199."

### Emoji substitutes

Aykah bans emoji, so AI sometimes substitutes:
- Bullet-point characters in caption ("• 280 GSM • Belgian linen • Solid oak")
- Sparkles or arrows ("→", "✦")
- ASCII art or pipes

All FAIL on Pinterest, IG, Reels caption. Keep prose.

### Founder ventriloquy

Bad (in voice of "Sanya"): "I'm so excited to share..."
Why: Real founders don't talk in marketing copy.
Fix: Either drop the "I" framing or write what Sanya would actually say in plain speech.

### Generic "modern" stacking

Bad: "Modern, minimal, and intentional design."
Why: All three words have lost meaning through overuse.
Fix: Show what's modern about the specific piece. "Low silhouette, no skirt, exposed oak leg."

## How to use this file

When drafting, scan the draft against this list before running the personas. If it contains any of the AI rhythm tells, rewrite first — don't waste persona attempts on rhythm fixes.

Personas will still catch some, but they're better used for taste/channel/founder-voice judgment than for rhythm hygiene.
