# Section Guide — how voice and structure flex per section

The brand voice is constant. Structure and tone-dial change per section.

The user names the section they're working on. Look up that section here and apply.

---

## PDP — Product Description Page

- **Structure:** 3-part — Hook (80–120 words) · Specs (60–100 words) · Care (60–80 words). Total 200–400 words.
- **Hook:** one declarative + one sensory line. Never a paragraph. Lead with how the piece feels in a room, then one material detail.
- **Specs:** named certifications (use `certifications-glossary.md`), exact GSM/dimensions, mill/country if known. Premium specs language from Voice Guide v2.
- **Care:** practical, brief. Disclose quirks honestly (linting, settling, patina) — premium brands disclose, mass brands hide.
- **Voice dial:** medium warmth, medium formality.
- **Required output:** title tag, meta description, alt-text per image.

## Category page

- **Length:** 60–120 word intro paragraph.
- **Structure:** one scene-setting line + one line on the category's craft signature + one line linking to the collection.
- **Voice dial:** medium-high warmth, medium formality.
- **Don't:** list every product. Don't sell. Set the room.

## Returns

- **Tone:** clear, transparent, never defensive. Open with generosity.
- **Structure:** numbers and dollar amounts as scannable headers/bold (14 days, 20% restocking, customer pays return shipping).
- **Verified facts (from brand-facts.md):** 14-day window from delivery, customer pays return shipping, 20% restocking if not in original packaging.
- **Don't:** lead with "we reserve the right to". Don't say "free returns" — they aren't.
- **Voice dial:** medium warmth, higher formality (statutory precision in calm rhythm).

## Shipping

- **Tone:** factual, generous, plain.
- **Structure:** numbers as headers — $199 threshold, $19.99 under, Canada-only. Lead with the complimentary line.
- **Verified facts:** complimentary delivery on orders $199 CAD and over; $19.99 CAD under; Canada-only shipping; no US, no international.
- **Don't:** say "free." Use "complimentary." Don't imply faster shipping than reality.
- **Voice dial:** medium warmth, medium formality.

## Warranty

- **Tone:** plain English, never lawyered. Premium brands are honest about coverage.
- **Structure:** length, who it covers, what's excluded — each as a scannable header.
- **Verified facts:** 1 year from purchase, original purchaser only (non-transferable), excludes normal wear / misuse / commercial use.
- **Don't:** imply lifetime coverage. Don't claim "industry-leading" or "best-in-class".
- **Voice dial:** medium warmth, higher formality.
- **Use FAQ format** for exclusions if prose gets brittle.

## FAQ

- **Format:** Q&A. Questions short, real-customer phrasing. Answers plain, factual, brand-voice consistent.
- **Bold:** dollar amounts, time periods, certification names.
- **Length:** 30–80 words per answer. Long answers signal the FAQ should be a separate page.
- **Order questions** by frequency or by funnel stage (pre-purchase, during, post-purchase).
- **Cross-link** to policy pages where the full answer lives — FAQ is summary, not source of truth.

## About / Brand story

- **Voice:** founder-direct, plain-spoken, no marketing department.
- **Anchor:** the gap Sanya & Salman were trying to fix as home stagers — beautiful pieces at outrageous prices, or affordable ones with zero service.
- **Values:** Comfort · Integrity · Approachability · Trust.
- **Reference set positioning:** quality of Article-grade + price of Structube-grade + cohesive collection + superior customer service.
- **Don't:** corporate origin tropes ("we set out to revolutionize..."), invented timelines, fake humility.
- **Voice dial:** higher warmth, medium formality.
- **Length:** 250–500 words. Long enough to feel real, short enough that someone reads it.

## Founder Letter

- **Voice:** first-person Sanya or Salman. Drop the formula entirely; keep the four voice attributes.
- **Anchor:** a specific moment, a specific decision, a specific reason. Not abstract values.
- **Sign-off:** name + role.
- **Don't:** ghost-write generic "founder thoughts". Don't open with "When we started Aykah..."  Pick a real moment.
- **Voice dial:** highest warmth, lowest formality of any section.
- **Length:** 200–400 words. Letters that are too long don't get read.

## Section not listed?

If the user names a section that isn't in this guide (e.g., privacy, contact, microcopy, error pages, post-purchase email), ask:

> "What's the primary goal of this section — informational, conversion, or brand-story? I'll adapt from the foundation."

Then apply the universal output rules from SKILL.md plus the closest analogue:
- Privacy → similar dial to Warranty (statutory precision, calm rhythm)
- Contact / Showroom → similar to Shipping (factual, generous, addresses + hours)
- Microcopy → tighter than any of these (action verbs, no filler)
- Email post-purchase → similar to Shipping (factual, useful, calm)

Don't invent a new template — adapt foundation + closest analogue.
