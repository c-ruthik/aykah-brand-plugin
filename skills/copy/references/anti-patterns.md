# Anti-Patterns — what NOT to write in long-form copy

Two layers: AI rhythm tells (machine-output cadence) + Aykah-specific brand drift.

The voice-gate agent catches the banned-words list mechanically. The 3-pass self-review catches tone drift. This file catches the rhythm and structural tells in between.

Scan every draft against this list before the self-review.

---

## AI rhythm tells (refuse to write)

### 1. Tricolons (rule of three)

Bad: "Beautiful, durable, and built to last."
Why: AI defaults to three. Real writing varies.
Fix: Pick one detail, name it precisely. "Built to last decades."

### 2. Em-dash overuse

Bad: "The Aura sectional — built for modern living — is now in stock."
Why: AI loves the parenthetical em-dash. Humans use it sparingly.
Fix: Two short sentences. "The Aura sectional. Now in stock."

### 3. "It's not just X — it's Y"

Bad: "It's not just a sofa. It's where you'll spend Sunday."
Why: This pattern is in every AI ad spec.
Fix: Just describe Y. "Where you'll spend Sunday."

### 4. "In a world where..."

Bad: "In a world where furniture is disposable, Aykah is built to last."
Why: Movie-trailer cadence.
Fix: Drop the framing. "Built to last."

### 5. "Imagine..."

Bad: "Imagine sinking into the Aura sectional."
Why: Doesn't trust the reader.
Fix: Show the scene. "The end of a long day. The Aura sectional, deep oat linen."

### 6. "Crafted with [vague claim]"

Bad: "Crafted with the finest materials."
Why: AI fallback when there's no real material name.
Fix: Name the material. "Belgian linen. Kiln-dried white oak."

### 7. Adjective stacks

Bad: "Beautiful, stunning, luxurious, modern oak coffee table."
Why: Each adjective dilutes the next.
Fix: One specific detail. "Solid white oak. Live edge."

### 8. "Welcome to..." / "Introducing..."

Bad: "Welcome to the new Aura collection."
Why: Press-release cadence.
Fix: Just state it. "The Aura collection, now at aykah.ca."

### 9. Closing summary ("In conclusion...")

Bad: "In conclusion, the Aura sectional truly represents..."
Why: Schoolwork cadence. AI's worst tic.
Fix: End on the strongest line. No summary.

### 10. Fake humility

Bad: "We don't claim to be perfect, but..."
Why: Performative.
Fix: Cut the qualifier. State what you actually do.

## Aykah-specific brand drift

### Banned-but-disguised

Even when the literal banned words aren't used, the *cadence* of a banned brand can leak in:

- "Save big on premium designs" → "save big" + "premium" cadence even without those exact words
- "Don't miss out on this beautiful piece" → urgency without "limited time"
- "Elevate your home with..." → "elevated living" rephrased

If the rhythm reads like a discount-store flyer or a Restoration Hardware press release, FAIL. The 3-pass review catches it.

### Defensive policy openings

Bad (Returns page): "We reserve the right to refuse returns that don't meet our criteria."
Why: Cold, lawyered. Premium brands open with generosity.
Fix: Open with the positive. "Returns within 14 days, in original packaging." Restrictions appear later.

### Material overclaiming

Bad: "Premium hardwood frame."
Why: "Premium" is banned and "hardwood" is vague.
Fix: Name the species. "Kiln-dried white oak frame." Or ask the user if you don't know.

### Inventing certifications

Bad: "GREENGUARD Gold certified upholstery."
Why: ONLY if the product actually carries it. Inventing is brand-suicide.
Fix: ASK. If unconfirmed, omit the certification claim entirely.

### Timeline filler

Bad (About page): "For years, Sanya and Salman have been on a mission to..."
Why: Stock biography cadence. Vague timeline.
Fix: A specific year, a specific moment. "When Sanya and Salman first walked the floor at a Vancouver showroom in 2020..."

### Generic "modern" stacking

Bad: "Modern, minimal, and intentional design."
Why: All three words have lost meaning through overuse.
Fix: Show what's modern about THIS piece. "Low silhouette, no skirt, exposed oak leg."

## How to use this file

When drafting:
1. Write the first pass.
2. Scan for AI rhythm tells (1–10 above) — fix any hits.
3. Scan for brand drift — fix any hits.
4. Run the 3-pass self-review (`review-checklist.md`).

If the draft hits 3+ rhythm tells, the underlying voice is wrong — start over rather than patching.
