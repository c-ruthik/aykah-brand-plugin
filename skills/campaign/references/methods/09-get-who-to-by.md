# Method 9 — Get/Who/To/By (Aykah application)

Source: ad-agency campaign brief discipline. Invoked when output type is `campaign`. Forces every campaign to clarify its actual goal in four lines.

## The structure

- **Get** — the specific behavior change we want
- **Who** — the specific person whose behavior should change (not a demo — a behavioral signal)
- **To** — the specific action they should take (measurable)
- **By** — the specific message or stimulus that will cause the action

If any of the four is vague, the campaign is vague. Force specificity.

## Aykah examples

### Example A — Spring event campaign (Burnaby showroom)

- **Get** — homeowners in the Lower Mainland who've browsed Aykah for 30+ days but haven't bought
- **Who** — current website visitors with sectional or dining table page views, located within 30 km of Burnaby
- **To** — RSVP to the spring "Reset Your Space" event at the Burnaby showroom
- **By** — telling them they'll get to sit on the new Liva, get free styling advice from Salman, and unlock a private member preview before public sale

### Example B — Aires Dining Chair Meta ad campaign (cold traffic)

- **Get** — design-conscious BC homeowners who shop online but haven't heard of Aykah
- **Who** — Pinterest/Instagram users 32–48 in BC with home-decor interests, saved 5+ furniture pins in last 30 days
- **To** — click through to the Aires PDP and request a free fabric swatch
- **By** — showing the chair in three different real customer dining rooms with the line "designed to wear in, not out"

### Example C — Email campaign to A-List members

- **Get** — repeat customers who've bought one piece in the last 12 months
- **Who** — A-List members with at least one prior purchase, opened last 3 emails
- **To** — book a private showroom visit for the new fall collection preview
- **By** — exclusive 48-hour preview access + a curated list of pieces selected to complement what they already bought

## Failure modes — flag these in any candidate brief

| Failure | Example | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Vague Who | "women interested in design" | Replace with behavioral signal: "saved 5+ furniture pins last 30 days" |
| Vague To | "feel inspired" | Replace with measurable action: "request a swatch" / "RSVP" / "click PDP" |
| Vague By | "by showing our quality" | Replace with concrete stimulus: "by showing 3 customer photos with the line '...'" |
| Vague Get | "build awareness" | Replace with specific behavior change: "shift comparison from Cozey to Aykah" |
| Get and To collapsed | "Get them to buy" | Get is the behavior change; To is the next-step action. Separate them. |

## Workflow when generating a campaign brief

1. Force fill all four lines.
2. For each line, ask: "Could two different people read this and pick different specific people / actions?" If yes, sharpen.
3. Cross-check the Who against the Premium Practical ICP (`02-icp.md`). Drift = drop.
4. Cross-check the By against the JTBD Forces (`01-jtbd.md`) — does it move Push, Pull, or Anxiety? Anxiety-reducing By's outperform Pull-amplifying ones for Aykah.
5. The brief is ready when each line is so specific you could find that exact person.

## Output integration

When generating type=`campaign` outputs, the Get/Who/To/By block goes at the top of the brief, before any creative concept or channel map.
