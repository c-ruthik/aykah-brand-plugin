# Method 8 — 5 Stages of Awareness (Aykah application)

Source: Eugene Schwartz, *Breakthrough Advertising* (1966). Invoked for `campaign`, `email-subject`, and `hook` output types. Determines what to say based on where the customer is in their awareness journey.

## The 5 stages

### Stage 1 — Unaware
Doesn't know they have a problem.

- **Say:** a story or surprising stat that creates the realization
- **Don't say:** anything about Aykah yet
- **Aykah cold-traffic example:** "Most $1,200 dining tables sold online are MDF with a wood-grain decal. By year 3, the veneer chips and they're landfill."
- **Channels:** cold Meta + TikTok ads, Pinterest reach campaigns, organic Reels

### Stage 2 — Problem-aware
Knows the problem (their existing furniture is wrong / wearing out / undersized), doesn't know solutions exist.

- **Say:** educational content explaining the solution category exists
- **Aykah example:** "Direct-from-factory furniture brands cut out the 60% retail markup. Here's how it works — and why most haven't expanded to Western Canada."
- **Channels:** YouTube, IG carousels, blog, Pinterest pin titles, top-of-funnel email

### Stage 3 — Solution-aware
Knows the solution category, comparing providers.

- **Say:** why Aykah specifically vs. alternatives. Comparison content lives here.
- **Aykah example:** "Cozey ships everything online with one showroom in Montreal. Aykah has showrooms in Burnaby and Mississauga where you can actually sit on it before you commit."
- **Channels:** retargeting Meta, Google Search, comparison pages, mid-funnel email

### Stage 4 — Product-aware
Knows Aykah's product, hasn't decided to buy.

- **Say:** social proof, specifics, risk-reversal. Testimonials, dimensions, returns policy.
- **Aykah example:** "Maria's had her Liva sectional 4 years. The bouclé still looks new. Here's her cat sitting on it. 30-day returns, white-glove."
- **Channels:** retargeting, abandoned-cart email, PDP, showroom-driving CTA, member preview email

### Stage 5 — Most aware
Ready to buy, just needs the final nudge.

- **Say:** facts about availability, lead time, price. Direct CTA. NO urgency tricks.
- **Aykah example:** "Aires Dining Chair, Moonlight Bouclé. Back in stock. $499. Ships from Burnaby in 5 days."
- **Channels:** abandoned-cart, post-showroom-visit email, member-only stock alerts

## The killer rule

**Matching message to stage beats clever copy at the wrong stage.** A brilliant Stage-1 ad shown to a Stage-4 customer wastes the impression. A mediocre Stage-4 ad shown to a Stage-4 customer converts.

When generating any candidate, name the stage it targets. If you can't, the candidate is generic.

## Aykah-specific channel × stage map

| Channel | Stages it serves | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Cold Meta + TikTok ads | 1–2 | Don't sell features. Sell the problem realization. |
| Pinterest pins (organic + paid) | 1–3 | Search-driven, save-driven. Stage-3 comparison pins outperform Stage-5 product pins. |
| Google Search | 3–5 | Bottom-funnel. Don't run Stage-1 here. |
| IG feed carousels | 2–4 | Education + comparison + social proof. |
| Reels / TikTok organic | 1–3 | Hook on problem realization, not feature claim. |
| Retargeting Meta | 3–4 | Stage-3 if not yet on PDP, Stage-4 if PDP-viewer. |
| Email — welcome series | 2–4 | Educate → compare → reassure. |
| Email — abandoned cart | 4–5 | Risk reversal first, urgency NEVER. |
| Email — A-List members | 4–5 | Skip earlier stages. They're already most-aware. |
| Showroom invite | 3–5 | Showroom is the conversion lever. Drive to it from any stage 3+. |

## When generating subject lines, hooks, or campaign creative

For every candidate, name:
1. The **stage** it targets
2. The **channel** it's intended for
3. The **action** it's pushing (matched to stage — Stage 5 is direct CTA, Stage 1 is realization)

A Stage-1 hook with a "buy now" CTA is broken. A Stage-5 email with a 200-word brand story is broken.

## Email subject lines — stage-specific patterns

| Stage | Subject line pattern | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Stage 2 | Educational, problem-naming | "Why most $1,200 dining tables won't last a decade" |
| Stage 3 | Comparison, alternative-framing | "Cozey vs. Aykah — what's actually different" |
| Stage 4 | Social proof, specifics, reassurance | "Maria's had her Liva for 4 years — here's how it held up" |
| Stage 5 | Direct, no-urgency, factual | "Aires Dining Chair — back in stock today" |

Avoid Stage-1 subject lines in email — your list is already past Stage 1 just by being on it.

## Common 5-Stages failures

- Showing Stage-5 urgency to Stage-1 audiences (cold traffic) — they're not ready
- Showing Stage-1 stories to Stage-5 audiences (members, abandoned cart) — they don't need education
- Skipping Stage 2 entirely — this is the highest-volume top-of-funnel slot most Aykah marketing leaves empty
- One subject line / hook for the whole campaign — match by audience, not by piece
- Treating "awareness" as a single bucket — five stages, five messages
