# Method 5 — Dunford Positioning (Aykah application)

Source: April Dunford, *Obviously Awesome* (2019). Loaded as grounding for every `/aykah:campaign` invocation. Defines the category Aykah plays in and what gets compared to what.

## The five components — applied to Aykah

### 1. Competitive alternatives

What customers would do if Aykah didn't exist:

- **Direct DTC + retail competitors:** West Elm, Cozey, Castlery, Article
- **Lower-price alternatives:** IKEA, Wayfair
- **Premium alternatives:** Rove Concepts, Crate & Barrel, Restoration Hardware
- **Non-purchase alternatives:** Facebook Marketplace, hand-me-downs, "we'll keep using what we have"
- **Full-service alternatives:** hire an interior designer

Each of these has different positioning needs. When generating campaign output, pick which ONE of these alternatives the output is competing against. Don't try to beat all five at once.

### 2. Unique attributes

What Aykah can provably deliver that the alternatives can't:

- **Physical showrooms in Burnaby BC + Mississauga ON** — Cozey has 1 (Quebec). Article has 0. IKEA has many but no DTC pricing. RH has many but at 3x the price.
- **Direct-from-factory pricing on solid hardwood** — most retail competitors are 2–3x markup. Most DTC competitors are MDF or veneered.
- **8 years of operating history** — most newer DTC competitors are 2–3 years old. Customers care about "will you still be here when I need a replacement cushion."
- **Showroom + online combined in Western Canada** — nobody else owns this lane.
- **Materials transparency on PDP** — visible joinery photos, hardwood specs, bouclé care guide. Most competitors hide this.

These are the only things you can claim about Aykah without inventing facts. Anything else needs to be backed by `brand-facts.md`.

### 3. Value (and proof)

Translate every attribute to a customer outcome ("so what?" three times):

| Attribute | Outcome (the thing the customer cares about) |
|---|---|
| Direct-from-factory | "Premium materials at half the markup" |
| Physical showrooms | "Sit on it before you commit. No fit anxiety." |
| Solid hardwood frame | "Lasts 15+ years not 3" |
| 8 years operating | "We'll still be here when you need a replacement cushion in 2030" |
| Materials transparency | "You see exactly what you're paying for — no hidden MDF" |

When generating any candidate, pick the **one outcome** the candidate delivers. If you can't name it, the output is generic.

### 4. Best-fit customer

The Premium Practical ICP (see `02-icp.md`). Not IKEA buyers, not full-budget designer clients, not pure-online <30 buyers, not contractor-flippers.

### 5. Market category frame

Three possible category frames Aykah could occupy. **Right now we are betting on Frame A.**

- **Frame A — "Showroom-backed direct-from-factory" (current bet)** — competing against Cozey/Article. Wins on showroom presence + breadth + history. ✅ This is the active frame.
- **Frame B — "Accessible designer furniture"** — competing against Rove/CB2. Wins on price. Reserve for future tier launch.
- **Frame C — "Modern showroom alternative to IKEA"** — competing against IKEA. Wins on quality + design.

Every campaign output should reinforce **Frame A**. Outputs that drift toward Frame B (premium-positioned, designer-coded) are off until the Fall 2026 premium tier launches. Outputs that drift toward Frame C (mass-market, value-coded) are also off — that's not where we're playing.

## How to use this in campaign generation

For every candidate:

1. Name the **competitive alternative** the candidate is competing against. (Cozey? Article? IKEA? Rove?)
2. Name the **unique attribute** the candidate is leveraging. (Showroom? Pricing? Materials?)
3. Name the **outcome** the candidate is selling. (Trust? Price? Longevity? Fit?)
4. Confirm the candidate fits **Frame A** (Showroom-backed DTC). If it drifts to Frame B or C, drop it.

If the candidate doesn't have clean answers to all four, it's not ready to ship.

## Aykah positioning statement (internal — not for marketing copy)

> For BC and Ontario homeowners furnishing their first or second home, Aykah is a direct-from-factory furniture brand with physical showrooms in Burnaby and Mississauga, that delivers solid-hardwood furniture at half the markup of retail competitors, with the trust and tactile confidence of a showroom — for buyers who would otherwise compromise between IKEA's price and Crate & Barrel's quality.

This is the internal compass. Don't paste it on the homepage. Use it to test whether candidate outputs align with the positioning.

## Common positioning failures

- Drifting into "luxury" framing (Frame B) before the premium tier launches
- Comparing to IKEA on price (Frame C drift) — that's not our category
- Calling ourselves "designer furniture" without the designer-trade infrastructure
- Treating all competitors as one bucket — Cozey, Article, IKEA, and RH each need different positioning angles
- Writing copy that could be from any DTC furniture brand — failure of category specificity
